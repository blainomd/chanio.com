The intelligence stack

Six AI layers.
Running simultaneously.
Always.

Most health AI is reactive — you ask, it answers. chanio is different. Six distinct AI layers run in parallel, feeding each other, updating your consciousness without you doing anything. Here is exactly what each layer does, which model runs it, and what it outputs.

Layer 1 Edge Intelligence gemma-3-nano · local
Always running · every page load · zero latency
A small local model runs inside the extension on your device. Every page you visit is classified in under 50ms — health topic, emotional tenor, urgency signal, role signal (are you researching as a patient, a caregiver, a clinician?). No data leaves your device. The edge layer produces a continuous stream of tagged signals that feed every layer above it. It runs whether you are on a health site or not — the absence of health browsing is itself a signal.
Outputs
topic_tags[] role_signal urgency_flag emotional_tenor condition_signal
Layer 2 Vault Intelligence claude-haiku · on upload
Triggered · fires within seconds of any document save
Every document you store — a lab result, a prescription, a photo of your discharge summary, a note you typed after a doctor visit — gets read immediately by Claude Haiku. It extracts structured medical events: medications with dosages, diagnoses with ICD codes, lab values with reference ranges, physician names, dates of service. It builds a personal medical ontology from your unstructured documents. When a new lab result contradicts a previous one, it flags the drift. When a new medication interacts with an existing one, it surfaces the conflict. It never loses context from earlier documents.
Outputs
medication_list condition_list lab_trends[] drift_flags[] ontology_update
Layer 3 Clinical Bridge fhir-parser + claude · on sync
On connection · then daily sync cadence
When you connect an EHR — Epic, Cerner, athenahealth, eClinicalWorks — the clinical bridge imports your structured record via FHIR R4. Problem list, medication list, active diagnoses, recent labs, upcoming appointments. Claude reads this clinical ground truth and cross-references it against the vault (Layer 2) and browsing signals (Layer 1). The critical output is the gap map: conditions you have been diagnosed with but have never browsed about. Medications on your list you have never looked up. Appointments you have rescheduled three times. The clinical bridge finds the distance between what your record says and what your behavior suggests.
Outputs
clinical_ground_truth gap_map[] adherence_signals appointment_flags
Layer 4 Synthesis Engine claude-sonnet · daily + trigger
Daily synthesis · plus trigger on significant signal shift
The only layer that sees all other layers simultaneously. Claude Sonnet receives the full output of Layers 1–3 — the behavioral stream, the vault ontology, the clinical ground truth — and synthesizes a coherent, human-readable consciousness summary. It asks three questions: Who is this person right now? What are they circling without acting on? What do they need that they have not asked for yet? The output is not a medical record. It is a narrative — readable by you, shareable with loved ones on your terms, compressed into a privacy-preserving signal for the network. This layer runs every 24 hours and whenever any other layer produces a major signal shift (a new diagnosis, a dramatic browsing pattern change, a new medication added).
Outputs
consciousness_summary circling_patterns[] unmet_needs[] network_signal
Layer 5 Family Graph claude-extended · parallel
Always parallel · runs alongside all other layers
You are not one person in one role. You might be simultaneously a patient managing your own hip pain, a caregiver researching your mother's memory loss, and a surgeon evaluating shoulder repair techniques. The family graph layer maintains separate but linked consciousness summaries for every person in your circle — and crucially, it knows which role you are occupying right now, in this session, on this page. When you visit hippain.help, the family graph tells the network you are there as a patient. When you visit memoryloss.help fifteen minutes later, it tells the network you are there as a caregiver. The signal changes. The site adapts. Your context is always correct.
Outputs
active_role care_circle[] per_person_summary role_signal
Layer 6 Community Intelligence claude + embeddings · on event
On post · on read · background feed updates
When you share an experience in the community, Claude validates it against your vault data — confirming the experience is real before it enters the corpus. It then anonymizes through semantic transformation, not simple redaction: the experience is rewritten to preserve meaning while removing identity. The result is a verified-anonymous post that carries the weight of real experience without exposing who you are. When you read, the community layer uses your consciousness summary (Layer 4) to score the entire corpus for resonance — which experiences match where you are right now, what condition you are managing, what decision you are circling. Your feed is a mirror of your current state, not a popularity algorithm.
Outputs
verified_anonymous_post resonance_feed[] corpus_contribution community_intelligence
